Infographic: The Queen's Village at Versailles Palace

The Queen's Village, also known as the Hameau de la Reine, is a charming rustic retreat located on the grounds of the Palace of Versailles, created for Queen Marie Antoinette in the 18th century. This small village was designed to allow her to escape the formality of the royal court and enjoy country life, far from the strict rules of the palace. The village was built with picturesque houses, a lake, and manicured gardens, emulating an idyllic countryside landscape.

The village includes several rustic-style buildings, such as the Le Cottage de la Reine, the Temple of Friendship, and several water mills. The queen and her court used to enjoy outdoor activities like boat rides on the lake and visits to the country houses. The space was a personal refuge for Marie Antoinette, allowing her to disconnect from the political and social pressures of the time.

Why visit the Queen's Village?

  • A break from the opulence of the palace: The Queen's Village offers a fascinating insight into the private life of Queen Marie Antoinette, in contrast to the grandeur of the Palace of Versailles.
  • A picturesque design: The landscape and rustic buildings allow visitors to experience a natural and serene environment, perfect for peaceful walks.
  • A place full of history: Visiting the village transports you to the time of the French Revolution, when Marie Antoinette sought refuge in this charming paradise, far from the tensions of the palace.
  • Connection with nature: The natural setting, with its lake, gardens, and mills, is perfect for enjoying the beauty of the landscape while learning about the queen's history.

Visit the Queen's Village and enjoy one of the most romantic and fascinating escapes in the history of France!
